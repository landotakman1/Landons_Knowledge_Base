For our purposes as they relate to modern gaming, we will skip over what I call the commercial game precursors like *Spacewar!* and *Tennis for Two*. The commercial video game really exploded in the arcades of the 1970s and early 1980s, then moved into the home through successive generations of competing consoles.

## Key Characteristics of the Arcade Era
- Short, intense sessions designed around repeated coin drops
- Extremely tight [[mechanics/Core Gameplay Loop|core gameplay loops]] that had to be fun within seconds
- High difficulty and leaderboard competition
- Hardware limited by what could fit in a cabinet and still turn a profit

Games such as *Pong*, *Space Invaders*, *Pac-Man*, and *Donkey Kong* established fundamental design patterns still visible today.

## Early Home Consoles
The move to [[history/The 3D Revolution & Modern AAA|living rooms]] (Atari 2600, NES, Master System, etc.) changed the design focus. Games could now be longer, support continues or passwords, and target a wider age range. The NES era in particular standardized the side-scrolling platformer and many genre conventions that later became mainstream. Games started focusing less on the coin drop short gameplay loop as gaming entered homes.

This period also saw the first major industry crash (1983) and the subsequent recovery led by Nintendo’s strict quality control - an early lesson in [[platforms/Console Ecosystems|platform ecosystems]] and curation.
