Independent (indie) games are created by individuals or small teams outside the traditional publisher-funded AAA structure. The modern indie boom began in earnest in the mid-to-late 2000s and continues today. However, independent developers and game enthusiasts have always been a crucial factor in gaming and many of the greatest ideas and advancments in gaming have come from these indie heroes.

## Enabling Factors
- Digital distribution platforms (Steam, itch.io, consoles’ digital stores)
- Affordable and powerful engines such as Unity and Godot
- Crowdfunding and early access models
- Social media and streaming as discovery channels

## Design Freedom
Without the need to satisfy a large publisher’s risk calculations, indie developers can take bigger creative risks. Many influential mechanics, art styles, and narrative experiments of the last fifteen years originated in the indie space before being adopted (or imitated) by larger studios.

Indie games have been especially strong in [[genres/Horror & Survival Games|horror]], experimental narrative, and tightly crafted short-form experiences. They also frequently demonstrate how strong [[mechanics/Core Gameplay Loop|core loops]] and clever systems can succeed without massive production values.

The indie sector has become both a vital source of innovation and a parallel industry with its own economic realities and challenges. In the last few years, indie game successes have actually rivaled and even overtaken the success of larger studios.
