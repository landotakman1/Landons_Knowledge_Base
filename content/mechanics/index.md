title: Gameplay Mechanics & Systems
---
These five interconnected resources explore the core mechanics that shape player experience. Together, these mechanics form a classification system with explicit relationships, demonstrating how organizing principles and intentional links create more value together than each element in isolation.

- [[Core Gameplay Loop]] - the repeating cycle of input, feedback, and choice that push player engagement
- [[Progression Systems]] - How players improve, discover content, and invest time.
- [[Resource Management Mechanics]] - The types of resources games use and potential trade-offs they create.
- [[Feedback and Rewards]] - How games communicate results and motivate players to keep playing.
- [[Player Agency and Choice]] - The feeling that player choices meaningfully shape game outcomes and environment.

These pages are purposefully linked to each other because mechanics in games never operate in isolation. Understanding the relationship between game mechanics is key to both understanding existing games and designing new ones.
