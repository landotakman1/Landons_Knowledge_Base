# Feedback and Rewards

## The Role of Feedback in Games
Feedback is how players receive responses from their actions in a game. Without clear feedback, players have no way to tell whether what they are doing is helpful or hurtful. The best games use many different channels for feedback including visual effect feedback, sound/audio feedback, changes in UI, and even the rumble in your controller.

## Reward Timing and Psychology
Rewards make players want to repeat the action that gave them the reward. This reinforces desired behavior in the game. These rewards include small, short-term rewards like that wonderful "Ping" of picking up coins, all the way to the larger, long-term rewards like unlocking new abilities or uncovering hidden plot areas in the main story. The best reward systems have a generous mix of both reward types.

> *"A game is a series of interesting choices." - Sid Meier*

This famous quote from the legendary mind behind the *Civilization* games (among others) highlights why feedback and rewards matter so much. If choices do not produce clear and meaningful feedback, they lose the player's interest. In the [[Core Gameplay Loop]], feedback is the step that closes the cycle and informs the players next decision.

## Positive vs. Negative Feedback Loops
Some games use positive feedback to create a sort of power snowball where player success makes future success increasingly more likely and plays well into the power fantasy that many players enjoy in a game. Alternatively, other games rely more on negative feedback where each player success results in sort of push-back from the game, thus increasing the challenge. Many co-op survival games use a generous mixture of positive and negative feedback to give players a sense of increasing power and ability while also elevating the challenge to keep the players interested and invested.

Clear feedback and properly paced rewards are key in making sure mechanical game systems evolve into memorable experiences that players look back on fondly. In turn, players search for these aspects in games they play in the future.
