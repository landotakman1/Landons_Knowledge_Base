# Player Agency and Choice

## What Agency Really Feels Like
Agency is the feeling a player gets when they can truly tell that their decisions meaningfully affect the game world and their own experience in the game. Agency is the main difference in the feeling between watching a movie or reading a book and playing a game. A good sense of agency makes players feel involved in both the successes and failures in the game world.

## Types of Agency in Game Design
Designers can create agency through:
- Meaningful choices with visible consequences
- Multiple possible paths to success
- Expressive and personalized customization (appearance, build, playstyle)
- Emergent gameplay resulting from game systems interacting in novel and unexpected ways

AAA RPG games typically represent agency in the most obvious form. Games like *Cyberpunk 2077* and *Red Dead Redemption 2* offer entirely different storylines and outcomes based on choices made by players throughout the story. Agency in these games makes players feel like they are writing the story as they play instead of simply existing in a pre-made world.

## Agency, Loops, and Relationships
Agency exists within the [[Core Gameplay Loop]]. If the game loop is too fixed, choices feel shallow. When [[Progression Systems]], [[Resource Management Mechanics]], and [[Feedback and Rewards]] all respond to player decisions, agency blooms. This is why all areas within this mechanics section are purposefully linked - they all describe an intimately interconnected web of game mechanics, not isolated features.

Games that honor player agency have a tendency to create the most memorable and loved player experiences.
