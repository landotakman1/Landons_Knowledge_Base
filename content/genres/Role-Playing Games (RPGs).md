Role-Playing Games place the player in a character (or party of characters) who grows in power and capability over a long period of time. Character progression, meaningful choices, and often a strong narrative are central to this genre.

## Core Pillars
- Character statistics, skills, or abilities that improve over time  
- Meaningful decision-making that affects the story or world  
- Exploration of a large world or series of interconnected areas  
- Combat systems that can range from turn-based to real-time

The long-term [[mechanics/Progression Systems|progression systems]] in RPGs are usually the strongest example of investment loops in all of gaming. Players repeatedly engage with the [[mechanics/Core Gameplay Loop|core loop]] because each cycle makes their character more powerful or opens new options or abilities.

## Major Traditions
- **Western RPGs** - Greater emphasis on player freedom and reactivity (*The Elder Scrolls*, *Fallout*, *Baldur’s Gate 3*)
- **Japanese RPGs (JRPG)** - Stronger authored stories and tend to include turn-based or highly stylized combat (*Final Fantasy*, *Persona*, *Dragon Quest*) - 
- **Action RPGs** - Blend real-time action combat with RPG progression (*Diablo*, *The Witcher 3*, *Elden Ring*)

## Relationship to Mechanics
RPG design is almost entirely defined by its systems. [[mechanics/Resource Management Mechanics|Resource management]] (mana, inventory, limited rests), [[mechanics/Player Agency and Choice|player agency]], and layered [[mechanics/Feedback and Rewards|feedback]] are usually more complex than in pure action games.

## Why RPGs Endure
The combination of personal growth, meaningful choice, and long-term investment creates a powerful sense of ownership. When done well, an RPG makes the player feel that the story and the character are partly their own creation.
