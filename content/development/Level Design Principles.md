Level design is the craft of shaping the spaces players move through and the challenges they encounter inside those spaces. Good level design teaches the player, controls pacing, and reinforces the game’s [[mechanics/Core Gameplay Loop|core gameplay loop]].

## Core Goals of Level Design
- Guide the player without constant explicit instruction or hand-holding
- Create interesting choices and routes
- Control tension, rest, and reward timing
- Teach new mechanics through environmental storytelling and safe practice spaces
- Support the fantasy and vibe of the game

## Common Techniques
- **Landmarking** - Distinctive visual features that help players orient themselves or provide an incentive to explore
- **Gatekeeping** - Soft or hard barriers that prevent players from reaching areas before they are ready (invisible walls $\neq$ good level design)
- **Risk/Reward spaces** - Optional dangerous paths that offer better loot or shortcuts
- **Pacing curves** – Alternating high-intensity and recovery sections

Level design is tightly connected to [[mechanics/Player Agency and Choice|player agency]] and [[mechanics/Resource Management Mechanics|resource management]]. A well-designed space makes the player feel clever for navigating it rather than frustrated by it and rewards players for taking risks.

## Iteration Is Everything
Almost all strong level design comes from HUNDREDS of total hours of repeated playtesting and adjustment. What looks good on paper often feels different when a real player moves through it.
