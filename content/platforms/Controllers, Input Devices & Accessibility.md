How a player gives input to a game is fundamental to how the game feels. Controllers, keyboards, mice, touch screens, and specialized devices all shape both design possibilities and who can play.

## Common Input Methods
- **Gamepad / Controller** - Standardized across modern consoles; excellent for movement and camera control  
- **Mouse & Keyboard** - Highest precision for aiming and complex commands  
- **Touch & Motion** - Common on mobile and some Nintendo hardware  
- **Adaptive & Specialized Controllers** - Designed for accessibility or specific genres (racing wheels, fight sticks, one-handed controllers)

## Impact on Design
Input devices directly affect [[mechanics/Player Agency and Choice|player agency]] and the kinds of [[mechanics/Core Gameplay Loop|core loops]] that feel good to players. A game designed for dual analog sticks will play differently than one designed for mouse aim.

## Accessibility
Modern design increasingly treats accessibility as a core requirement rather than an afterthought. Options such as remappable controls, one-stick modes, subtitle customization, colorblind support, and difficulty assistance expand who can enjoy a game without changing its fundamental identity.

Thoughtful input and accessibility design is one of the clearest ways a development team shows respect for its audience.
