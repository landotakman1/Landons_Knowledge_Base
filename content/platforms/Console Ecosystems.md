[[Arcade Era & Early Consoles|Modern consoles]] are more than just hardware. They are closed ecosystems that combine hardware, online services, exclusive games, and curated user experiences.

## The Major Players
- **PlayStation** - Strong first-party narrative and action-adventure focus  
- **Xbox** - Emphasis on Game Pass, backward compatibility, and cross-platform play  
- **Nintendo** - Unique hardware concepts and first-party family-friendly (and experimental) software

Each ecosystem shapes the kinds of games that thrive on it. Exclusive titles often showcase the strengths of that platform’s controller, services, or design philosophy.

## Design Implications
Console games are typically designed around a [[Controllers, Input Devices & Accessibility|standard controller and a living-room or handheld context]]. This influences everything from [[mechanics/Core Gameplay Loop|core gameplay loops]] to user interface decisions and play session length. Performance targets (frame rate and resolution modes) are also more standardized than on PC.

## Why Ecosystems Matter
Choosing a console is often a choice of community, exclusives, and services as much as raw hardware power. Understanding these ecosystems helps explain why certain [[genres/index|genres]] and design approaches dominate on certain platforms.
