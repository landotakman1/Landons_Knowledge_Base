PC gaming is defined by flexibility, high performance potential, and a culture of customization. Unlike consoles, the PC is an open platform where hardware, software, and input methods can be mixed freely and the gamer can truly personalize their experience.

## Key Characteristics
- Wide range of hardware configurations (from low-end laptops to extreme enthusiast builds)  
- Support for mouse and keyboard as primary input, [[platforms/Controllers, Input Devices & Accessibility|plus various controller types and styles]]  
- Easy [[community/Modding Culture|modding and community content]]  
- Simultaneous release or earlier access to many games  
- Backwards compatibility through software rather than hardware generations

The openness of the PC platform has a direct effect on game design. Many [[mechanics/index|gameplay systems]] that rely on precise aiming or complex interfaces originated on, or are best experienced on, PC.

## Strengths and Trade-offs
**Strengths**: Graphical fidelity, frame rates, mod support, multitasking, massive libraries through digital storefronts. 
**Trade-offs**: Greater complexity for new users, varying performance, and the need to manage drivers and settings not fully present on consoles.

## Relationship to Other Topics
PC gaming has historically driven innovation in [[genres/index|genres]] that benefit from precise control (strategy, FPS, simulation) and remains the primary home of serious [[community/Modding Culture|modding culture]].
