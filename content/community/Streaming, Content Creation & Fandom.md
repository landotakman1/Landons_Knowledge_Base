Gameplay is no longer experienced only by the person holding the controller. Streaming platforms, highlight clips, and fan communities have made games a shared cultural performance.

## Major Forms
- Live streaming (Twitch, YouTube Live, Kick)
- Edited video content and long-form analysis
- Fan art, music, theories, wikis, and memes
- Community-run events, speedrunning, and collaborative challenges

This layer of culture changes how games are discovered, evaluated, and remembered. A title can succeed commercially because it is entertaining to *watch* as much as it is to play. Designers increasingly consider how moments will look and sound on stream, how easily clips can be shared, and how the game supports or resists community creativity.

Fandom also creates secondary organizing systems: wikis, tier lists, fan databases, and lore archives that sit alongside the original game and sometimes become essential resources in their own right.
