Esports refers to organized, competitive video gaming watched by an audience. While competitive play has existed since the arcade era, the modern esports industry grew rapidly with online connectivity, streaming, and professional team structures.

## Requirements for Strong Competitive Games
- Deep skill ceiling so that mastery remains interesting
- Clear, readable [[mechanics/Feedback and Rewards|feedback]] so both players and spectators understand what is happening
- Meaningful [[mechanics/Player Agency and Choice|decision-making]] under pressure
- Balance that prevents a single strategy from dominating indefinitely
- Strong spectator experience (camera tools, observer modes, broadcast integration)

Games such as *Call of Duty*, *League of Legends*, *Counter-Strike*, *Dota 2*, and *Valorant* have built entire ecosystems of teams, leagues, and freelancers around these principles.

Competitive play also feeds back into design: many mechanical decisions in multiplayer games are made with both the top-level competitive scene and the broader casual audience in mind.
