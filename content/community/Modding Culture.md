Modding is the practice (an art really... if you know, you know) of players altering or extending a game’s content, systems, or presentation after release. It is one of the clearest examples of players becoming co-creators.

## Forms of Modding
- Cosmetic and quality-of-life changes
- New levels, campaigns, or story content
- Total conversions that turn one game into something almost unrecognizable
- Tool-assisted or community-made editors released by the original developers

PC gaming has historically been the center of modding because of easier file access and a culture that expects it. Some games (*Skyrim*, *Minecraft*, *Doom*) have multi-decade modding communities that keep them alive long after official support ends.

## Impact on Design and Longevity
Active modding communities can dramatically extend a game’s lifespan and sometimes influence official updates or sequels. *Skyrim* is probably one of the most obvious examples of this where you can watch ten different streamers play the game and all ten look and play vastly differently.

Modding also raises interesting questions about ownership, authorship, and the boundary between player and developer.
